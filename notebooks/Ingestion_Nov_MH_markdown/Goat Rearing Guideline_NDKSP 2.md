# Goat Rearing Guidelines
## Introduction:
Climate change is observed to have an adverse impact on the state's agriculture, and the scope of this impact is expected to increase in the future, as noted in the state's Climate Change Action Plan. Farmers in Marathwada and Vidarbha have been facing severe droughts for the past few years, which has adversely affected groundwater reserves and soil health. Consequently, the productivity of agricultural crops has decreased. The Nanaji Deshmukh Krishi Sanjivani Prakalp has been launched by the Government of Maharashtra with financial assistance from the World Bank, with the objective of empowering farmers to adapt to the situation arising from climate change.
The "Goat Rearing" component has been included under the Nanaji Deshmukh Krishi Sanjivani Prakalp to provide supplementary income to landless families in the project area and to generate employment in rural areas.
## Objectives:
1. To provide supplementary income to landless individuals, widows, deserted, and divorced women in the villages included in the Nanaji Deshmukh Krishi Sanjivani Prakalp through the "Goat Rearing" component.
2. To provide self-employment opportunities to the youth in rural areas.
3. To rear breeding goats of species that can thrive in the local environment.
4. To promote the goat rearing business in rural areas.
## Beneficiary Selection Criteria:
1. Landless individuals, widows, deserted, and divorced women in the villages selected under the project will be eligible for this component.
2. The benefit of this component can be availed by only one person from a family.
3. If the applicant beneficiary or any other member of their family has previously availed the benefit of this component from any other government scheme, they will not be eligible to receive the benefit under the project.
4. It is necessary for landless individuals to submit a certificate of being landless from at least the Tehsildar.
5. It is necessary for widows, deserted, and divorced women to submit a certificate from the competent authority to avail the benefit.
## Included Components and Financial Assistance in the Scheme:
1. Under this component, it is mandatory to purchase a goat unit consisting of 4 goats and 1 buck.

2. When purchasing a goat unit, it is necessary that the breeding does are in their second or third lactation and the breeding buck is at least 1 year old and above.
3. It is mandatory to have the purchased goat unit insured for 3 years under this component.
4. A $75 %$ subsidy on the approved norms will be permissible for this component.

# General Instructions
1. The beneficiary should have adequate space and shelter for rearing the goats.
2. It is mandatory for the beneficiary to purchase the goat unit only from the Punyashlok Ahilyadevi Sheep and Goat Development Corporation.
3. It is mandatory for the beneficiaries to purchase a unit of 4 breeding does and 1 buck of breeds like Osmanabadi/Sangamneri or other breeds that can thrive in the local environment. The beneficiary will have the freedom to choose the breed of goats.
4. It is mandatory to purchase the said goat unit in the presence of the purchase committee.
5. The cost of purchasing the goat unit, its insurance, and transportation will have to be borne by the beneficiary.
6. It will be mandatory to tag the purchased goats and register them on the Bharat Pashudhan portal (NDLM-National Digital Livestock Mission) with the help of an officer from the Animal Husbandry department.
7. Under this component, the beneficiaries will be provided with vaccination and necessary veterinary services for the purchased goats through the nearby veterinary institution. The Taluka Agriculture Officer should plan for this with the help of an officer from the Animal Husbandry department.
8. If any violation of the guidelines is found, responsibility will be fixed on the concerned officer, and administrative action will be taken.
9. The beneficiary should be made aware that if serious discrepancies are found in fulfilling the responsibilities mentioned in the guidelines, or if it is proven that the benefit received under the project has been misused, action can be taken against the concerned beneficiary as per the terms and conditions in the application and undertaking.
10. The implementation of this component should be done meticulously to ensure that its benefits reach the neediest farmers.

# Responsibilities at Various Levels in Implementation-
## Beneficiary
1. Interested individuals should register online on the project's website, submit the application, and upload the necessary documents.
2. The goat unit should be purchased within two months of receiving the presanction.
3. The selected beneficiary should purchase the goat unit in the presence of the constituted purchase committee.
4. The beneficiary should purchase the goat unit from the Punyashlok Ahilyadevi Sheep and Goat Development Corporation and submit the receipt with the purchase details.
5. The transportation cost after the purchase of the goat unit will have to be borne by the beneficiary.
6. Under this component, the insurance for the purchased goat unit should be taken in the joint name of the beneficiary and the concerned Taluka Agriculture Officer for 3 years.
7. After the purchase of the goat unit, an online application for the subsidy should be made, and the self-attested original purchase bills, purchase committee's certificate, insurance receipt, and veterinary officer's certificate should be uploaded by the beneficiary to the website.
8. It will be mandatory for the beneficiary to show the goats to the officials of the Agriculture Department whenever they visit for verification of the goat unit.
9. It will be the complete responsibility of the beneficiary to get the purchased goats vaccinated against necessary diseases from the nearby veterinary hospital and to deworm them.

3. To conduct a social audit of the implemented component.

# Taluka Agriculture Officer
1. To scrutinize the application received from the beneficiary, the documents uploaded with the application, the minutes of the Village Agriculture Development Committee meeting, and the site inspection report and photos from Desk-2.
2. To verify whether the said beneficiary or any member of their family has availed the benefit of this component from any other government scheme. For this, coordinate with the Animal Husbandry Department.

5. If deficiencies are found in the application during scrutiny, the said application should be sent back to the previous desk through the DBT system by noting the deficiencies.
6. If the beneficiary is not found eligible as per the scheme's guidelines, the said application should be rejected for pre-sanction.
7. To organize training for the beneficiaries on this component with the help of officials from the Animal Husbandry Department, in coordination with the Project Director, ATMA, and the Sub-Divisional Agriculture Officer.

8. To periodically verify that the implementation of this component in your jurisdiction is being done as per the guidelines. Also, to complete the supervisory inspection as prescribed.
Purchase Committee:- It is mandatory for the beneficiary to purchase the goat unit from the Punyashlok Ahilyadevi Sheep and Goat Development Corporation in the presence of the purchase committee.
The structure of the purchase committee is as follows.
1. Taluka Agriculture Officer - Chairman
2. Animal Husbandry and Dairy Business Officer/Animal Husbandry Development Officer (Extension) of the concerned taluka, Panchayat Samiti - Member
3. Head of the veterinary hospital in the jurisdiction - Member
4. Insurance representative - Member
5. Beneficiary
6. Circle Agriculture Officer - Member Secretary
# Functions and Responsibilities of the Purchase Committee:-
1. The goat unit of breeding does of Osmanabadi/Sangamneri or other breeds should be purchased from the Punyashlok Ahilyadevi Maharashtra Sheep and Goat Development Corporation.
2. To provide technical assistance to the beneficiary in purchasing a high-quality goat unit, in coordination with the Punyashlok Ahilyadevi Maharashtra Sheep and Goat Development Corporation.
3. The purchase committee should submit a certificate stating that the beneficiary has purchased the goat unit in person.
4. To ensure that the purchased goats are tagged and registered on the Bharat Pashudhan portal (NDLM-National Digital Livestock Mission).
5. To assist the beneficiary in purchasing another goat from the insurance money if the allotted goat dies.
6. All the selected beneficiaries in the village should preferably purchase the goat unit at the same time.
