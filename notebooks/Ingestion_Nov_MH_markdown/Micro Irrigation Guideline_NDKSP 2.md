# Guidelines for Micro-Irrigation Component
## Introduction:
The adverse effects of climate change are visible on the agriculture sector of the state, and it is mentioned in the state's Climate Change Action Plan that the extent of these effects will increase in the future. Farmers in the Marathwada and Vidarbha regions have been facing severe droughts for the past few years, leading to adverse effects on groundwater reserves and soil health. Consequently, the productivity of agricultural crops is decreasing. The Nanaji Deshmukh Krishi Sanjivani Project has been initiated by the Maharashtra Government with financial assistance from the World Bank with the objective of enabling farmers to adapt to the situation arising from climate change.
The Central Government has issued Operational Guidelines in the year 2024 for implementing the Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana - Per Drop More Crop, a centrally sponsored micro-irrigation scheme. This scheme is being implemented from the year 2022-23 under the name Rashtriya Krishi Vikas Yojana - Per Drop More Crop. The Commissionerate of Agriculture, vide letter no. JA.KR. KUA/FALO4/SUSIYO/MA.SU./26359 dated 25/10/2024, has issued guidelines for the microirrigation scheme. On the lines of this scheme, a micro-irrigation scheme (Drip and Sprinkler Irrigation) will be implemented under the Nanaji Deshmukh Krishi Sanjivani Project. Accordingly, the following guidelines are being issued for implementing the approved micro-irrigation component in the villages selected under the project.
## Objectives:
1. To increase the area under micro-irrigation by using modern technology in the project districts and to promote climate-resilient farming practices.
2. To encourage micro-irrigation methods for the development of agriculture and horticulture crops based on modern technology.
3. To manage fertilizers through the micro-irrigation system.
4. To increase agricultural production, the quality of agricultural produce, and consequently, the total income of farmers.
5. To increase water use efficiency.
## Beneficiary Selection Criteria:
1. Under the project, for the micro-irrigation component, beneficiaries will be selected from farmers holding up to $$ hectares of land in the priority order of Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes, Women, Persons with Disabilities, and General category.
2. The farmer should have a $7 / 12$ and 8 -A extract in their own name as proof of land ownership.

3. It is necessary for the farmer to have irrigation facilities.
4. A beneficiary farmer with a joint landholding must submit a consent letter from the other co-holders, which should be on plain paper.
5. A beneficiary farmer will be eligible for a subsidy for a maximum area of $$ hectares.
6. If a farmer has previously availed subsidy for an area less than 5.00 hectares, they can avail the subsidy for the remaining area, up to the 5.00 -hectare limit.
7. If a farmer has previously availed subsidy for micro-irrigation on a particular plot of land, they will be eligible for the subsidy again for the same micro-irrigation component on the same plot of land after a period of 7 years.
8. 8. If a farmer has availed the benefit of the Portable Sprinkler (movable sprinkler set) component under the micro-irrigation scheme, the said farmer can avail the benefit of a drip irrigation set on the "same field" after three years. The subsidy will be provided for the drip irrigation set as per the Effective Differential Subsidy. (A farmer who has availed the benefit of a micro-sprinkler or mini-sprinkler will not be eligible for a subsidy for drip irrigation on the same field after three years).

# A) Implementation Procedure:
## 1. Applying:
All interested farmers should apply through the project's website (https://dbt-ndksp.mahapocra.gov.in/).
## 2. Special Instructions for Applicants:
I. The applicant must accurately fill in all information such as area, survey number, village name, etc., as per the $7 / 12$ and 8-A extracts while applying.
II. If incorrect information is provided while filling the application, the application will be cancelled. Therefore, the information should be filled carefully.
III. For Drip / Mini Sprinkler / Micro Sprinkler irrigation types, while applying online, the crop spacing dimension is given in "meters". The applicant should select one of the available spacing options.
IV. Since the subsidy will be payable within the limits of the pre-sanction amount based on the information provided by the applicant in the application, accurate information must be filled in the application.
## 3. Uploading Documents:
I. If the applicant belongs to Scheduled Caste / Tribe or Persons with Disabilities categories, a valid certificate must be uploaded.
II. It is mandatory for a beneficiary farmer with a joint landholding to submit a consent letter from the other co-holders, which should be on plain paper. (Appendix 8) III. A quotation from an authorized dealer of a registered micro-irrigation manufacturing company.
## 4. Scrutiny of Application / Documents, Granting Pre-sanction, and Other Responsibilities Cluster Assistant
1. The Cluster Assistant will get the applications filled out by interested beneficiary farmers on the project's portal https://dbt-ndksp.mahapocra.gov.in/
2. After the farmers apply online, the Cluster Assistant will scrutinize the documents uploaded by the farmer.
3. All online applications received will be presented to the GKVS for approval, and further action will be taken according to the decisions made in the committee meeting.
4. They will explain the terms and conditions of the scheme, as well as the layouts and technical specifications of drip/sprinkler systems to the farmers.

# Gram Krushi Vikas Samiti (GKVS)
1. They will create awareness among interested farmers in the village to apply for the benefits of components under this scheme.
2. They will scrutinize all online applications received and the uploaded documents with the help of the Cluster Assistant and select beneficiaries according to the priority order of Scheduled Caste, Scheduled Tribe, Women, Persons with Disabilities, and General category.
3. They will make a decision on the eligibility/ineligibility of the applications received from interested beneficiaries and inform the ineligible applicants about the reasons for their ineligibility.
4. They will conduct a social audit of the implemented components.
## Assistant Agriculture Officer
1. Scrutinize the documents uploaded online by the applicant.
2. While scrutinizing, check the farmers' online applications, the minutes of the GKVS meeting, and other related documents.
3. Verify the data of beneficiaries who have availed benefits in the last seven years from the E-Thibak Legacy Data system, as well as the information of beneficiaries who have availed benefits from 2020-21 onwards from the available data in the Benefit History of the MahaDBT portal.
4. Check whether the beneficiary farmer has previously availed benefits from other schemes for the proposed area in the application. If benefits have been availed previously, recommend the eligible area for pre-sanction after verifying the area for which the benefit was taken.
## Circle Agriculture Officer
1. Periodically verify that all procedures in the field including beneficiary selection and disbursement of subsidies are being carried out as per the guidelines.
2. Visit at various stages while the work is in progress to provide technical guidance to the beneficiary and ensure that the work is being done as per technical specifications.
3. Periodically review the implementation of the said component in the jurisdiction.
4. Conduct supervisory inspections as prescribed in the guidelines.
## Taluka Agriculture Officer

1. The scrutiny of documents and applications in the proposals recommended for pre-sanction by the Assistant Agriculture Officer will be done by the Taluka Agriculture Officer.
2. Pre-sanction shall be given only after verifying the data of beneficiaries who have availed benefits in the last seven years from the E-Thibak Legacy Data system, as well as the information of beneficiaries who have availed benefits from 2020-21 onwards from the available data in the Benefit History of the MahaDBT portal.
3. Ensure that the farmer has uploaded an undertaking online stating that they have not received a duplicate subsidy for the same area within a period of seven years (Appendix 7).
4. If any farmers' applications or documents are incomplete, the proposals for which prior approval cannot be granted due to lack of compliance shall be sent back to the Agriculture Assistant Officer for rectification of the deficiencies.
5. The Taluka Agriculture Officer will reject the application if the beneficiary is found to be ineligible as per the guidelines.
6. If a farmer's application is rejected without any valid reason, action will be taken against the concerned official under Rule 10 of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Discipline and Appeal) Rules, 1979.
7. Conduct supervisory inspections as prescribed in the guidelines.
# 5. Purchase of Micro Irrigation Set and Claiming Subsidy Beneficiary:
1. The applicant should submit a quotation from an authorized dealer of a microirrigation manufacturing company registered with the Commissionerate of Agriculture, Pune, for their district while applying.
2. It will be mandatory for the farmer to purchase the drip/sprinkler set of their choice only from a manufacturer registered at the Commissionerate of Agriculture level and from a dealer registered at the District Superintendent Agriculture Officer's office.
3. The list of manufacturers registered at the Commissionerate of Agriculture level and dealers registered at the District Superintendent Agriculture Officer's office is available on the Agriculture Department's website.
4. The farmer should conduct a site visit with the company's technical representative. Based on the water test report provided by the farmer, water availability, diesel/electric motor capacity, soil type, crop to be taken, land slope, distance of the proposed drip/sprinkler set from the water source, etc., and after

a physical inspection of the area, the company representative shall prepare a layout plan.
5. It will be mandatory for the beneficiary to purchase the micro-irrigation set, install it, and make it operational within 30 days of receiving the pre-sanction.
6. The farmer should procure the materials from the dealer and get the set installed as per the layout and design provided by the company's technical representative.
7. The farmer should also obtain the layout plan along with a certificate signed by the company representative stating that the layout is technically sound and costeffective.
8. The farmer should submit (upload) a scanned copy of the invoice (with GST number) and the layout plan, countersigned by the company representative, on the project's portal.
9. The farmer should also keep one copy of the invoice for their own records.
10. After receiving pre-sanction and before installing the set, it is necessary for the farmer to enter into an agreement on plain paper with their chosen manufacturing company or its representative. (The prescribed format is enclosed in Appendix 9.)
11. If a farmer has installed the micro-irrigation set before receiving pre-sanction and then submits a proposal for a subsidy, such proposals will not be considered eligible for the subsidy.

# Embossing/Carving on Sprinkler Irrigation System Pipes:
1. To prevent the sprinkler irrigation set from being brought from another farmer during the physical inspection of the beneficiary farmer's set, and thus to prevent subsidy being given twice for the same set, the following text should be embossed on the female part of the pipe joints of the sprinkler set.
2. The following text should be embossed on the female part of the pipe joints of the portable sprinkler set: "Farmer's Name, Village, Year of Installation." This text should be clearly and legibly carved by the dealer before handing over the set, in a way that it can be read with normal eyesight. After carving, it should be colored. The text should preferably be carved using a simple machine. The text should be such that it cannot be easily erased.
3. The field officer should verify that this text has been written during the on-site inspection and record it in the on-site inspection report. If the aforementioned embossing is not found during the on-site inspection, such proposals should not be recommended for subsidy payment.
4. If a sprinkler irrigation set is found to have been supplied without embossing, a report should be submitted by the Deputy Agriculture Officer to the Taluka Agriculture Officer. The Taluka Agriculture Officer should issue a notice to the concerned dealer to complete the embossing within 3 days. If the dealer fails to do the embossing even after a total of 3 such notices, a recommendation to cancel the dealer's registration should be made to the District Superintendent Agriculture Officer's office. The District Superintendent Agriculture Officer should blacklist the concerned dealer, forfeit the bank guarantee, and not renew their registration for the following year.
5. A manufacturer/company-wise list of CML numbers has been made available on the e-Thibak portal's on-site inspection report. During the physical on-site inspection, verification should be done as per the CML numbers mentioned in the said list.
6. Applications of farmers deemed eligible after scrutiny will be forwarded by the Deputy Agriculture Officer to the Accounts Officer (Sub-Division level).

7. If discrepancies are found in the farmer's invoice or set layout, or if incorrect information is filled while uploading the invoice, such applications will be sent back online to the farmer for rectification.
8. Proposals of farmers whose applications have deficiencies that cannot be rectified and are thus deemed ineligible will be rejected by the Deputy Agriculture Officer with recorded reasons.
9. If a farmer's application is rejected without any valid reason, action will be taken against the concerned official under Rule 10 of the Maharashtra Civil Services (Discipline and Appeal) Rules, 1979.
